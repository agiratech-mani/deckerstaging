Course Curriculum

Q: How will this course help me?

A: You’ll learn how to become a better communicator. This course will change the way you think about what you say and how you say it, whether you’re standing in front of the room for a big presentation, chiming in during a seated meeting, interrupting on an important phone call, sending an email to your boss, or even talking to your kids. It’s packed with specific, actionable tips that you can use right away.

Q: Looking at the modules, I can’t tell what’s covered. What does this course entail?

A: This course will focus on the communication experience, and it is designed to help you become an even better communicator. We’ll examine both what you say and how you say it.



We’ll start with the “how you say it” part, and you’ll learn where to look (and for how long), what to do with your hands, etc. We’ll also identify how you can use your voice to convey credibility.



Then, we’ll get to the “what you say” part, where you’ll learn how to create content that is focused and action-oriented. Plus, we have a time-tested method that will help you save time doing it. You’ll also learn how to be memorable.



Throughout the course, there are many opportunities to practice and watch your progress. It’ll be fun - and jam-packed. Get ready to work!

Q: What is the Decker Digital Lab?

A: The Decker Digital Lab is where you will go to test, drill and show off your new skills. Think of it as the tinkering studio where you can practice, adjust and improve. We’ve got a variety of “live” audiences ready to simulate what it’s like to speak to a group of people. Whether it’s one-on-one, a small meeting or a large group, it’s always more nerve-racking to talk to real people than to practice in front of the mirror. So we’re bringing the people to you, in a fun, safe way. They’ll be listening - and maybe even jotting down a note or sipping some coffee to keep them awake. Your goal is to keep your audience tuned in!

Q: What does “Behaviors of Trust” mean? 

A: We’ll explain it all in Module 3 and 4. But here’s a sneak peek: The way that we come across when we speak, the way that others perceive us and the habits that we exhibit when we are communicating are all ways we experience the Behaviors of Trust. In this course, we have separated the Behaviors of Trust into two categories: ways to create connection and ways to create credibility.

Using My Work Station

Q: What’s the Work Station all about?

A: Your Work Station is where all of your notes, videos and content are stored throughout the course. You’ll see a few different sections:

  • My Notepad: Here’s where you can take notes during the course. It’s also the place where we will save all of your answers, goals and responses. As a bonus, we’ve also prepared a few notes for you from each module. You can print them all out at the end of the course.
  • My Grid™: This tab contains your completed Decker Cornerstones™ and Decker Grid™. Not sure what the Decker Cornerstones and Decker Grid are yet? Don’t worry - it will make sense as you go along (to be more specific, you’ll get to it in Module 5). You can also print your Decker Cornerstones and Decker Grid after you complete them.
  • My Videos: This tab is where each of your recordings of yourself will be stored. They’ll stay here for 12 months, or until you reset the course to take it again.
  • My Resources: Here’s where we will keep some handy-dandy reference materials for you. Check it out (and if you don’t see anything there, yet, check back during a later module - we tried to time everything just perfectly).

Q: What does it mean to have the light on my face?

A: You don’t have to rent a spotlight. Here’s the thing: we want to see you, not a silhouette! Make sure the light is coming from in front of you, like a lamp shining on your face or overhead lights that illuminate a whole room.
   

If you can’t see your face in the webcam test, physically move or adjust the lights until you can see your face.

Recording Issues

Q: I just watched my first video and it’s awful. How can I re-record?

A: We know it’s not easy to see yourself on video. 99% of people hate it. But trust us, these videos are the best way to identify your habits so that you can improve your communication skills. The good news is, it’s also really easy to re-record a video if you flub, get star-struck with our “live” audience, or want to practice again. Just hit the back arrow button until you’re back in The Lab and you can record again.

Q: What if I want to re-record all my videos?

A: We love the commitment to continuous improvement! Once you’ve take the course as designed, the modules are unlocked and you can revisit and re-record in any section and any module you like. Sky’s the limit for number of times you can re-record - though each time you re-record, you will lose your original recording.

Q: The course keeps freezing or buffering slowly. What do I do?

A: Ugh. Sorry to hear that. Here are a few things to try:

  • Turn your Wifi off and on to reconnect.
  • Close other applications running on your computer (including other browser windows).
  • Restart your computer. Don’t worry, it will save your place.
  • Update your browser and flash plugin to the latest settings.
  • If you’re taking this course through your company, call your IT support person.
  • If you’re taking this course directly from Decker, email support@decker.com
